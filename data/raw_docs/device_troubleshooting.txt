## 1. Troubleshooting Philosophy: The "Tier 1" Essentials

Before escalating or processing a warranty claim, all agents (and AI modules) must ensure the "Core Four" steps have been completed. These resolve approximately 65% of reported issues.

1. **Power Cycle:** Turn the device off for 30 seconds and restart.
2. **Toggle Airplane Mode:** Turn Airplane Mode ON for 10 seconds, then OFF to force a fresh connection to the nearest tower.
3. **Check for Updates:** Ensure both the Operating System (iOS/Android) and Carrier Settings are up to date.
4. **Reseat SIM Card:** If using a physical SIM, eject and re-insert it to ensure the gold contacts are clean and properly aligned.

## 2. Network & Connectivity Issues

### 2.1 "No Service" or "Searching"

If a device fails to connect to the WEE Mobile network:

* **Coverage Check:** Cross-reference the customer’s GPS location with the **WEE Coverage Map**.
* **SIM Status:** Check if the SIM is "Active" in the billing system. A "Soft-Hotline" status (due to non-payment) will cause a "No Service" error.
* **Network Selection:** Go to *Settings > Mobile Network > Network Selection* and ensure it is set to **Automatic**.
* **APN Settings:** For non-WEE branded devices (Bring Your Own Device - BYOD), ensure the APN is set to `internet.weemobile.com`.

### 2.2 Slow Data Speeds

Slow data is often a result of environment or plan limitations rather than device failure.

* **FUP Check:** Determine if the customer has exceeded their high-speed data threshold (refer to the Billing Policy).
* **Bandwidth Type:** Check the status bar.
* **5G/5G+:** Optimal.
* **LTE/4G:** Standard.
* **E/2G/3G:** Indicates poor signal or tower maintenance.


* **VPN Interference:** Ask the customer to disable any active VPNs or "Private Relay" (iOS) and re-test speeds.

### 2.3 Wi-Fi Calling Issues

WEE Mobile supports Wi-Fi Calling for improved indoor coverage.

* **Requirements:** A valid E911 address must be registered on the WEE Mobile account.
* **Troubleshooting:** If calls drop on Wi-Fi, ensure the internet connection has at least **2Mbps** upload speed.

## 3. Calling & Messaging (Voice/SMS)

### 3.1 Dropped Calls / Poor Voice Quality

| Symptom | Potential Cause | Fix |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Call Fails Immediately** | VoLTE Disabled | Enable "Voice & Data" in Cellular settings. |
| **Choppy Audio** | Local Interference | Move away from large electronic appliances or thick concrete walls. |
| **Calls go to Voicemail** | Do Not Disturb (DND) | Check if DND or "Silence Unknown Callers" is active. |

### 3.2 SMS/MMS Failures

* **Short Code Blocks:** If a customer cannot receive 2FA codes (from banks, apps), check the account for "Premium SMS Blocks."
* **MMS Size:** WEE Mobile limits MMS attachments to **1MB**. Larger files should be sent via RCS (Android) or iMessage (Apple).
* **iMessage Conflicts:** If a customer recently switched from iPhone to Android, they must deregister iMessage to receive texts from other iPhone users.

## 4. Battery & Power Management

### 4.1 Rapid Battery Drain

AI agents should look for the following "Battery Killers":

1. **Background Refresh:** Suggest disabling background activity for high-usage apps (e.g., social media, navigation).
2. **Brightness:** Ensure "Auto-Brightness" is enabled.
3. **Signal Strain:** If the device is in a "Low Signal" area, the battery drains faster as it searches for towers. Recommend Wi-Fi Calling in these zones.

### 4.2 Device Overheating

* **Environment:** If the device is hot to the touch, check if it was in direct sunlight or charging while using high-performance apps (gaming).
* **Safe Mode:** If the device overheats without a clear cause, boot into **Safe Mode** (Android) to determine if a third-party app is the culprit.

## 5. Physical & Hardware Assessment

### 5.1 Water Damage

WEE Mobile does not cover liquid damage under the standard 12-month warranty.

* **LDI (Liquid Damage Indicator):** Instruct the customer to check the LDI (usually found inside the SIM tray slot).
* **White/Silver:** No damage.
* **Pink/Red:** Liquid contact detected.


* **Action:** If LDI is red, refer the customer to **WEE Protect Insurance** or a paid repair.

### 5.2 Screen & Display Issues

* **Unresponsive Touch:** Remove screen protectors or cases that may be interfering with the digitizer.
* **Dead Pixels:** If the screen has vertical lines or black spots, this is a hardware failure. If there is no visible crack, it may be covered under warranty.

## 6. WEE Mobile Warranty & Repair Policy

### 6.1 Standard 12-Month Warranty

WEE Mobile covers manufacturer defects for **one year** from the date of purchase.

* **Covered:** Spontaneous hardware failure, battery swelling, charging port failure (without debris), software "bricking."
* **Not Covered:** Cracked screens, water damage, "jailbroken" software, or damage from non-WEE chargers.

### 6.2 The "Dead on Arrival" (DOA) Window

If a device is defective within **14 days** of purchase, it qualifies for a "New-in-Box" replacement. After 14 days, the device will be replaced with a "Certified Pre-Owned" (CPO) unit of equal or greater value.

## 7. AI Agent Decision Logic (Flowchart Data)

To help your AI agent make decisions, use the following logic:

> **IF** Problem = "Connectivity"
> **THEN** Run "Network Reset" command.
> **IF** Problem persists **AND** LDI = White **AND** Age < 12 months
> **THEN** Initiate Warranty Exchange.
> **ELSE** Refer to WEE Protect Insurance.

> **IF** Problem = "Billing Related Service Cut-off"
> **THEN** Redirect to Payment FAQ (Document WM-FAQ-BILL-2025-002).

---

## 8. Final Escalation Protocol

If all troubleshooting steps fail:

1. **Backup Data:** Remind the customer to backup to iCloud or Google Drive.
2. **Factory Reset:** This is the last step before hardware replacement. Warning: This erases all user data.
3. **Ticket Creation:** Note the specific error codes and the steps already attempted to ensure the Tier 2 human technician does not repeat the same work.
