# WEE Mobile: Consumer Billing Policy & Terms of Service

**Document ID:** WM-POL-BILL-2025-001

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025

**Version:** 4.2

---

## 1. Introduction and Purpose

The purpose of this document is to outline the billing practices, payment requirements, and financial responsibilities of customers subscribed to WEE Mobile services. This policy ensures transparency in how charges are calculated, invoiced, and collected. By subscribing to WEE Mobile services, the customer agrees to the terms set forth in this policy.

## 2. Billing Cycles and Invoicing

WEE Mobile operates on a **monthly recurring billing cycle**.

### 2.1 Cycle Assignment

Upon activation, customers are assigned to one of four billing cycles (beginning on the 1st, 8th, 15th, or 22nd of the month). This date remains the customer’s "Bill Anniversary Date."

### 2.2 Invoice Delivery

* **Paperless Billing:** By default, WEE Mobile utilizes electronic invoicing (e-Bill). Notifications are sent via SMS and email.
* **Paper Invoices:** Customers may request physical paper statements for a monthly administrative fee of **$3.50 per statement**.

### 2.3 Proration

In the first month of service, or when changing plans mid-cycle, charges are prorated. This means customers are charged only for the days the service was active. Data allowances are also prorated accordingly.

## 3. Payment Terms and Methods

All invoices are due **21 calendar days** from the invoice date (the "Due Date").

### 3.1 Accepted Payment Methods

WEE Mobile accepts the following:

* **Credit/Debit Cards:** Visa, MasterCard, American Express, and Discover.
* **Direct Debit:** ACH transfers from verified checking or savings accounts.
* **WEE Wallet:** Digital credits applied via the WEE Mobile App.
* **In-Store Payments:** Cash or card payments at authorized WEE Mobile retail locations.

### 3.2 Auto-Pay Program

Customers enrolled in Auto-Pay receive a **$5.00 monthly discount** per line. Funds are automatically withdrawn 2 days prior to the Due Date. If an Auto-Pay transaction fails, the discount is forfeited for that billing cycle.

## 4. Late Payments and Penalties

WEE Mobile provides a **5-day grace period** following the Due Date.

### 4.1 Late Fees

If payment is not received by the end of the grace period, a late fee of **$15.00 or 1.5% of the outstanding balance** (whichever is higher) will be applied to the subsequent invoice.

### 4.2 Service Suspension (Soft-Hotline)

Accounts more than 30 days past due are subject to "Soft-Hotline" status. In this state:

* Outgoing calls (except 911 and WEE Support) and data services are restricted.
* Incoming calls and SMS remain active.
* A **$35.00 Reconnection Fee** applies to restore full service.

### 4.3 Collections and Debt Recovery

Accounts remaining unpaid for 90 days will be permanently disconnected. The remaining balance, including any equipment installment balances, will be referred to a third-party collection agency.

## 5. Usage-Based Charges and Overages

While most WEE Mobile plans include "Unlimited" data, specific thresholds apply based on the Fair Usage Policy (FUP).

### 5.1 Data Overages

On "Lite" plans (non-unlimited), data consumed beyond the monthly allotment is charged at **$10.00 per 1GB**. Customers receive SMS alerts at 50%, 80%, and 100% of their data usage.

### 5.2 Premium Services

Charges for third-party services (e.g., premium SMS contests, app store purchases charged to mobile) will appear in a separate section of the invoice titled "Third-Party Pass-Through Charges."

## 6. International Roaming and Long Distance

Charges for usage outside of the WEE Mobile domestic network are billed in arrears and may appear on the invoice up to two billing cycles after the usage occurred.

### 6.1 Roaming Zones

* **Zone 1 (Canada & Mexico):** Included in "Global Plus" plans; otherwise, $5.00/day.
* **Zone 2 (Europe & Asia):** $10.00/day for the "TravelPass" or Pay-As-You-Go rates of $0.25/min and $2.00/MB.
* **Zone 3 (Rest of World):** Pay-As-You-Go rates only.

### 6.2 International Long Distance (ILD)

Calls made from the domestic network to international destinations are charged per minute unless an ILD Add-on is active.

## 7. Taxes and Regulatory Surcharges

Invoices will include mandatory government taxes and regulatory fees, which vary by the customer’s primary place of use (PPU). These include:

* Federal Universal Service Fund (USF).
* State and Local Sales Tax.
* 911 Emergency Service Fee.
* Regulatory Cost Recovery Fee (a WEE Mobile administrative fee).

## 8. Disputes and Refunds

### 8.1 Dispute Window

Customers must notify WEE Mobile of any billing errors or disputed charges within **60 days** of the invoice date. Failure to dispute within this window constitutes acceptance of the charges.

### 8.2 Investigation Process

WEE Mobile will investigate disputes within 15 business days. During an active investigation, the customer is not required to pay the disputed portion of the bill, but must pay the undisputed balance to avoid late fees.

### 8.3 Refund Policy

Approved refunds are typically issued as **Bill Credits**. Cash or original-payment-method refunds are only issued in the event of account closure or overpayment of a final bill.

## 9. Account Closure and Final Bill

Upon cancellation, the final bill is issued on the next regular Anniversary Date.

* **No Proration on Cancellation:** WEE Mobile does not prorate the final month of service. Customers are encouraged to cancel near the end of their cycle.
* **Equipment Installment Plans (EIP):** If a customer has an outstanding balance on a device, the full remaining balance becomes due immediately upon account closure.
